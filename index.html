The Stops, in Detail

🌿 Hallim Park (한림공원)

Admission ~ ₩15,000 · mostly covered

A 100,000 m² botanical and cultural park founded in 1971 — lava caves, tropical plants, a traditional village, and an extensive bonsai collection, all on mostly flat walking paths. English signage throughout.

Natural & Geological Features

  • Hyeopjae Cave (협재굴) — A lava tube with a rare combination of lava-cave and limestone-cave features (white stalactites). The interior stays cool (~17°C) year-round.
  • Ssangyong Cave (쌍용굴) — "Twin Dragon Cave," another lava tube connected to Hyeopjae by a short walking path.

Gardens

  • Subtropical Botanic Garden (아열대식물원) — Hundreds of tropical and subtropical plant species, originally planted in 1971 on what was barren sand.
  • Water Garden (연못정원) — Ponds built on natural lava rock, with water lilies, lotuses, and a large waterfall.
  • Jeju Stone and Bonsai Garden (제주석·분재원) — Over 300 bonsai specimens (some over a century old) arranged alongside Jeju volcanic stones. Especially popular among international visitors.

Cultural & Heritage

  • Jae-am Folk Village (재암민속마을) — Traditional Jeju thatched houses (초가집) with basalt stone walls, arranged as a lived-in village from the early 20th century.
  • Jae-am Stone Exhibit Hall (재암수석관) — A collection of Jeju suseok (natural stone art), an East Asian tradition of appreciating stones with poetic forms.

Other Highlights

  • Bird Garden (사파리조류원) — Peacocks, ostriches, and other species in open enclosures.

🏖️ Hyeopjae Beach (협재해변)

Free · outdoor · ~ 5 min walk from the park

Widely considered Jeju's most scenic beach — white sand, shallow turquoise water, and the small volcanic islet of Biyangdo (비양도) rising offshore. A cluster of cafés lines the beach.

What to Do

  • Walk the shoreline — Roughly 1 km of flat sand and a short wooden boardwalk.
  • Biyangdo (비양도) island view — The small volcanic islet offshore is the beach's signature backdrop and a favorite subject for photographs.
  • Café break — Several well-known cafés with beach views, including Hotel Sand and Comma — see below.

Nearby Cafés (5–10 min walk)

  • Hotel Sand (호텔샌드) — A landmark café with sand-covered outdoor terrace and direct beach view.
  • Comma (콤마) — Minimalist interior with panoramic windows facing Biyangdo.
  • Hyeopjae-i-gil (협재이길) — Quieter café a block inland, popular for tea and desserts.
